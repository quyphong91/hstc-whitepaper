01 — The Problem

HS Code Classification is Hard,
Slow, and High-Stakes

Every product a company imports or exports must carry a specific 8-digit HS code. Getting it wrong is not an inconvenience — it triggers customs delays, financial penalties, and audit exposure.

◈

What is an HS Code?

The Harmonized System (HS) is a standardized numerical method for classifying traded products, maintained by the World Customs Organization (WCO). In Vietnam, every import/export declaration must include an 8-digit HS code that determines: tariff rates (MFN, FTA discounts), value-added tax obligations, import/export permits and licensing requirements, and trade statistics reporting.

Current Pain Points

📚

Massive Reference Volume

The Vietnam Tariff Schedule has 97 chapters and thousands of headings. The Explanatory Notes span thousands of pages. No one can memorize all of it.

🔍

Multi-Source Verification Required

Correct classification requires cross-referencing the Tariff Schedule, WCO Explanatory Notes, Vietnam Supplementary Notes, and recent rulings — all in different formats and locations.

⏱

Time-Intensive Manual Process

A single unfamiliar product can take an experienced professional 30–60 minutes of research. For companies processing dozens of new SKUs monthly, this is a significant bottleneck.

💸

High Cost of Errors

Misclassification leads to: post-clearance audits, overpaid or underpaid tariffs, missed FTA preferential rates, and shipment holds that disrupt supply chains.

🧠

Knowledge Locked in Individuals

Classification expertise lives in the heads of a few experienced staff. When they leave, institutional knowledge goes with them. There is no systematic way to capture it.

📋

Ruling Changes Are Easy to Miss

Vietnam Customs Authority (TCHQ/CHQ) issues hundreds of rulings per year. Keeping up with them requires constant monitoring — most teams simply cannot do this manually.

Design Philosophy: Evidence-Based, Not Decisive

HSTC presents the most relevant legal evidence — tariff descriptions, explanatory note excerpts, and official rulings — so that the qualified professional can make an informed, defensible classification decision. The AI layer provides ranked recommendations with explicit citations, not black-box answers.

03 — Data Foundation

Four Official Legal Sources,
Pre-Compiled and Indexed

Every result HSTC returns is sourced from official, authoritative documents. The platform does not generate or invent classification guidance — it retrieves and presents what the law says.

01

Vietnam Tariff Schedule

The official 8-digit HS code listing for Vietnam, covering all 97 chapters and approximately 5,000+ headings and subheadings. Includes MFN tariff rates, VAT rates, and applicable notes for each code.

97 Chapters 5,000+ HS Codes MFN Rates VAT Rates
02

WCO Explanatory Notes (EN)

The authoritative international interpretation guide published by the World Customs Organization. These notes explain the scope and boundaries of each heading — what is included, what is excluded, and why. Available in both English and Vietnamese.

Bilingual EN/VI WCO Official Heading Scope Inclusion/Exclusion
03

Vietnam Supplementary Explanatory Notes (SEN)

Vietnam-specific supplementary notes that extend the WCO guidance with local context, domestic product definitions, and Vietnam Customs Authority interpretations specific to the Vietnamese tariff schedule.

Vietnam-Specific Local Context VCCA Guidance
04

Classification Rulings (TB-CHQ)

Official classification rulings issued by the General Department of Vietnam Customs (TCHQ/CHQ). These decisions on specific products create binding precedents that customs officers and importers must follow. Updated continuously from official government sources.

Official Rulings Binding Precedents Auto-Crawled Continuously Updated
↻

Automated Ruling Updates

HSTC includes an automated crawler that continuously indexes new classification rulings from the official Vietnamese legal database (thuvienphapluat.vn). Rulings are parsed, stored, and indexed automatically — so the knowledge base stays current without manual effort.

04 — Search Intelligence

Three Layers of Search,
Each Smarter Than the Last

Users can choose the level of analysis they need — from instant keyword results to full AI-powered classification with cited legal reasoning. Each layer builds on the one above it.

Layer 1

Keyword Search — BM25 Algorithm

Type product keywords in Vietnamese or English. The BM25 ranking algorithm scores all HS headings by relevance against the Tariff Schedule, Explanatory Notes, and Rulings simultaneously. Vietnamese diacritics are handled automatically — typing without accents works just as well.

Vietnamese & English Accent-insensitive All 4 sources Relevance-ranked
<100ms instant
Layer 2

Semantic Search — AI Embeddings

When keywords miss the mark, semantic search finds matches based on meaning. The system converts both the query and all legal documents into AI vector embeddings, enabling concept-level matching. A search for "đế giày" (shoe sole) will surface relevant headings even if those exact words don't appear in the tariff description.

Concept matching AI embeddings 9,700+ indexed chunks
~2s fast
Layer 3

AI Classification — GPT-4o Analysis

For a full classification analysis, the AI tool accepts a product name, description, material, and intended use — then runs a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline. It retrieves the most relevant legal context from BM25 and semantic search, then asks GPT-4o-mini to produce a ranked list of HS code candidates with explicit, cited reasoning in Vietnamese.

RAG pipeline Bilingual query expansion Cited reasoning Ranked candidates
~15–30s full analysis

Step-by-Step: AI Classification in Action

STEP 01

Describe the Product

Enter product name, full description, material composition, and intended use. More detail = better accuracy.

STEP 02

AI Expands the Query

GPT generates bilingual technical search terms (Vietnamese + English) to maximize coverage across all legal sources.

STEP 03

Retrieve Legal Context

BM25 + semantic search retrieves the most relevant tariff headings, Explanatory Note excerpts, and prior rulings.

STEP 04

AI Classifies with Citations

GPT-4o-mini analyzes the product against retrieved legal context, applies GRI rules, and outputs ranked HS codes with explicit citations.

STEP 05

Professional Reviews

The qualified team member reviews the AI recommendation, reads the cited evidence, and makes the final classification decision.
