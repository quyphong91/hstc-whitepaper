01 Context

The Problem Worth Solving

Every product a business imports or exports must be assigned a correct HS Code (Harmonized System Code) — a globally standardised number that determines tariff rates, regulatory controls, and compliance obligations. Getting it wrong is not a minor inconvenience.

What is an HS Code?

Think of it as a product's "international tax ID number." The same code is recognised by customs authorities in 200+ countries. A 6-digit HS Code narrows down to a specific product category; Vietnam extends this to 8 digits for national tariff purposes. The code on your import declaration determines your duty rate, VAT eligibility, licensing requirements, and inspection exposure.

Scale of the Challenge

The HS Nomenclature contains over 5,000 headings and tens of thousands of sub-headings. Their legal descriptions, exclusions, and classification notes span thousands of pages — in multiple languages.

🕐

Time Pressure

Customs declarations are time-sensitive. Delays incur storage fees, demurrage charges, and broken delivery commitments. Researching the correct code manually can take hours per product.

💸

Cost of Misclassification

Under-declaring duty rates triggers back-payment demands, administrative fines, and reputational risk with customs authorities. Over-declaring inflates costs and erodes margins.

🔄

No Systematic Tool

Most teams rely on personal experience, spreadsheet lookups, or paid consultant advice. There is no scalable, searchable, always-updated tool integrated into the enterprise workflow.

05 Layer 1

Keyword Search — Fast, Precise, Always Available

The first and fastest search layer. A user types a word or phrase — in Vietnamese or English — and the engine scans all HS notes and tariff descriptions in milliseconds, returning ranked results with highlighted matching text.

Analogy

Keyword search works like the search function in a very well-organised legal reference library. You type what your product is made of, what it does, or what it's called — and the system finds every paragraph in the official legal notes that mentions those words, ranked by how relevant each paragraph is.

Why It Handles Vietnamese Well

Vietnamese is a tonal language written with diacritical marks. In practice, users often type without full accent marks — especially on mobile or when using unfamiliar keyboards. The normalisation layer strips all diacritics before matching, so vải dệt thoi and vai det thoi produce identical results. This is a deliberate design choice that dramatically reduces "no results" failures.

Search Filters

🔬

Material Filter

Restrict results to headings that classify goods by what they are made of — useful when the product's material is the primary classification criterion (e.g. plastics, metals, textiles).

🎯

Exact Phrase

Require the query to appear as a continuous phrase, not just as individual words scattered across the note. Reduces noise for very specific product names.

📄

Has Explanatory Notes

Show only headings that have detailed EN text available. Useful when the user specifically wants to read the legal reasoning, not just the tariff name.

✅

Perfect Match

Show only results where every search token appears in the matched text — the strictest filter, suitable for well-defined product names.

06 Layer 2

Semantic Search — Finding Meaning, Not Just Words

Keyword search is powerful for known product names, but it fails when the user's description does not share vocabulary with the legal text. Semantic search solves this by matching on meaning — understanding that "heat-resistant padding" and "thermal insulating material" describe similar things even when they share no common words.

Analogy

Imagine describing a product to a colleague — not using the official name, but by explaining what it does. A keyword search engine would fail because your words don't match the legal text. A semantic engine understands intent, the same way a knowledgeable colleague would understand you and point you to the right heading anyway.

What Is a Meaning-Vector?

Simple Explanation

Every piece of text — a product description, an HS note paragraph, a tariff name — can be converted by an AI model into a long list of numbers (a "vector"). Similar meanings produce similar number lists. "Dog" and "puppy" would produce very similar vectors. "Dog" and "aircraft engine" would produce very different ones. The system uses this mathematical similarity to find HS notes whose meaning closely matches a product description — regardless of word choice.

🔤

When Keyword Search Falls Short

A product called "corrugated cellulose packaging insert" shares few exact words with HS notes for heading 4823 (other articles of paper/paperboard). Semantic search bridges this gap by recognising the underlying concept — processed paper product used in packaging.

⚡

Pre-Computed at Build Time

Converting text to meaning-vectors is computationally intensive. The platform does this once, at build time, for the entire legal corpus. At query time, only the incoming description needs converting — making semantic search nearly as fast as keyword search.

07 Layer 3

AI-Assisted Classification — Full Analysis for Complex Cases

For products that are genuinely ambiguous — multi-component goods, new technologies, processed materials — the AI classification layer provides a full, reasoned analysis. It reads the most relevant HS notes, applies classification logic, and produces a ranked list of candidate headings with written justification.

Key Distinction

The AI does not guess. It is given the actual legal text relevant to the product — sourced by the search API — and asked to apply classification principles to that text. The result includes the specific paragraphs of law the AI relied on, making the output auditable and reviewable by a human officer.

What the Output Contains

🏆

Ranked Heading Candidates

The AI returns its top heading candidates in order of likelihood, not just one answer. For a complex product, it may return 2–4 plausible headings with confidence notes on each.

⚖️

Written Reasoning

For each candidate, the AI explains in plain language why that heading fits — and why others were considered but ranked lower. This is the "thinking" that a senior customs expert would normally provide.

📖

Cited Legal Text

The specific paragraphs from the Explanatory Notes or Supplementary Notes that the AI used are shown alongside the result. The officer can read the original source and form their own independent judgment.

📋

Admin Review Queue

All AI classification requests pass through an admin review queue before being marked complete. This provides a human quality-control gate and allows the compliance team to flag unusual classifications.

Usage Controls

AI classification is computationally and financially more costly than keyword or semantic search. The platform enforces a daily quota per user (50 requests/day) to ensure fair access and cost predictability. Quotas are tracked server-side in the database — not just client-side — making them tamper-resistant. This control mechanism is fully configurable for enterprise deployment.

How It Scores Results — BM25

The API uses the BM25 algorithm — the same relevance scoring method used by enterprise search engines like Elasticsearch and Apache Solr. It is not a simple word-count; it rewards term specificity (rare, precise words score higher than common ones) and term density (a paragraph mentioning the query term five times scores higher than one that mentions it once). This produces reliably relevant results without requiring AI computation at query time.

1

Query Arrives

A search query arrives from any caller — the AI pipeline, an ERP system, or a developer. The caller specifies the language (Vietnamese or English) and how many results to return.

2

Text Normalised

The query is lowercased, accent marks are stripped (critical for Vietnamese), and common stop-words are removed — ensuring consistent matching regardless of how the user typed their query.

3

BM25 Scoring Across All Paragraphs

Every paragraph in the HS legal corpus is scored against the query. The scoring is fast because the index is pre-computed — the API does not read the raw text documents at query time.

4

Grouped by Heading

Multiple matching paragraphs from the same 4-digit heading are consolidated into a single result. The highest-scoring paragraph becomes the "evidence snippet" — what the AI reads or what the user sees highlighted.

✓

Ranked JSON Response Returned

A structured JSON list of heading codes, sorted by relevance score, each with its matched evidence snippet and score value. The caller can choose how many results to consume.
